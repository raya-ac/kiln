# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We want Kiln to be a place where people can ask questions, file bugs, and send
patches without having to brace themselves first. Everyone participating in
this project — issues, pull requests, discussions, private mail — agrees to
make that possible.

## In practice

**Do:**

- Be patient with people who know less than you, and generous with people who
  know more.
- Give feedback on the work, not the person.
- Assume good faith on the first pass. Ask before concluding.
- Credit others' ideas when you build on them.

**Don't:**

- Harass, insult, demean, or threaten anyone. This includes jokes "about" a
  group you're not in.
- Out someone, share their private information, or pressure them to disclose
  anything about themselves.
- Derail unrelated threads with off-topic political or personal arguments.
- Keep doing something after you've been asked to stop.

This applies to everything connected to the project: GitHub, any chat rooms
we open, conference talks, DMs that reference the project — anywhere you're
representing Kiln or interacting with its community.

## Reporting

If someone's behaviour is making the project unsafe or unpleasant for you,
email **conduct@raya.li**. Reports are read by the maintainers only and are
not shared further without your consent.

We'll respond within a few days and tell you what (if anything) we're going to
do. Possible outcomes range from "a private word" to "permanent ban from all
project spaces", depending on severity and pattern.

## Scope

Maintainers apply this code consistently — to contributors, to users, and to
ourselves. If you think a maintainer has fallen short, say so; we'd rather
hear it than not.
